How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by starting with the end goal in mind and then working backward to figure out what pieces are needed to get there. For this project, I thought about what kind of scene I wanted to create and then broke it down into smaller parts like objects, lighting, textures, and camera movement. Instead of trying to build everything at once, I focused on one piece at a time and made sure each part worked before moving on.

What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me develop a better understanding of spatial design and how different elements fit together in a 3D environment. I also improved my ability to think about user perspective, especially with camera movement and how a scene is experienced visually. Another skill I built was attention to detail, because even small changes in positioning or lighting had a big impact on the overall look of the scene.

What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed a step-by-step process where I first created the base scene, then added objects, then applied textures, and finally worked on lighting and refinement. After getting everything placed, I spent a lot of time adjusting positions and scaling to make the scene look more realistic. Lighting was one of the last major steps, since it depends on everything else being in place.

How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The biggest takeaway I can apply in the future is breaking complex problems into smaller pieces and building them step by step. That approach works in almost any type of software development. I also learned the importance of refining and revisiting design decisions instead of assuming the first version is good enough.

How do I approach developing programs?

When developing programs, I focus on writing small, manageable sections of code and testing them as I go. I try to keep things modular so that functions can be reused and adjusted without affecting everything else. I also rely heavily on debugging and testing frequently instead of waiting until the end.

What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One new strategy I used was separating responsibilities into functions like transformations, materials, and textures. This made it easier to apply consistent changes across multiple objects. I also focused more on using structured data like materials and light sources to control how objects behave instead of hardcoding everything.

How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration played a huge role in this project. Almost nothing worked perfectly the first time, especially object placement and lighting. I had to constantly make adjustments, test the scene, and then refine it again. That process helped me understand how different parts of the scene interact with each other.

How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?

At the beginning, I was more focused on just getting things to work. As I progressed through the milestones, I started thinking more about organization, readability, and reusability. By the end of the project, I was much more intentional with how I structured my code and how different components worked together.
